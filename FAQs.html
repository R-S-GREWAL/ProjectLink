Frequently Asked Questions

Do children have better survival rates than adults with cancer?

Childhood cancer survival rate has changed over the last five years for difficult-to-treat diseases, which calls for innovative treatments with new methods to control resilient and resistant diseases.

What does the golden ribbon symbolise?

Gold is very precious thing and since the children are our future and our most precious treasure, it is a perfect icon representing the importance of defeating childhood cancer. The gold ribbon represents all types of cancer affecting children and teens, it is the universal icon to raise awareness about childhood cancer increase attention towards it. But unfortunately there isn't much awareness about what gold ribbon represents. It is unfortunate and shows, more should be done to bring awareness about childhood cancer.

What is our community doing to raise awareness about the childhood cancer?

Every September during Childhood Cancer Awareness Month, members of our community give their support, open their voice to the campaign and engage in discussions to bring awareness to the challenges of childhood cancer. All social media channels (Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn etc.) fill with discussions and engagements to raise awareness. The hashtags are introduced to take part in these discussions: #ChildhoodCancerAwarenessMonth #ShineGold etc.

How common is childhood cancer?

Despite of improving survival rates, cancer is still the most common disease-related cause of death of children in the world

How long is the treatment for childhood cancer lasts?

Treatment for childhood cancers differs based on their type. Some kinds require a single surgery, for some it requires several rounds of radiation, and for others mean months of chemotherapy or stem cell transplant and recovery. Treatment for leukemia, the most common childhood cancer, often ranges from several months to more than two years.
