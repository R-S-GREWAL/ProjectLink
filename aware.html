Childhood cancer awareness and Steps taken by Government

In 2018, World Health Organization(WHO) launched the Global Initiative for Childhood Cancer, to provide leadership and technical assistance
to governments for supporting them in building and sustaining high-quality childhood cancer programmes. The goal is that to achieve at least
60% survival for all children with cancer by the year 2030. It represents an approximate doubling of the current cure rate and will save an at least
additional one million lives over the next decade.



After three years WHO with partnership of St Jude Children's Research Hospital initiated the Global Platform for Access to Childhood Cancer
Medicines, the first of its type, to provide a better supply of quality-assured childhood cancer medicines with support from selecting to
dispensing medicines according to best possible care standards.

International Childhood Cancer Day



Childhood Cancer International is a network of parent organizations that spans continents, proclaimed International Childhood Cancer Day to ensure
that children with cancer can get the best care possible. This day is set aside to raise awareness about childhood cancers and their
treatments among the people. As mentioned earlier Every year, more than 400,000 children, teenagers, and young adults under the age of 19 are diagnosed
with cancer. While cancer is one of the leading causes of death from disease among children, the actual number of children who die because
of cancer has reduced over the years but this figure is of the identified cases.
