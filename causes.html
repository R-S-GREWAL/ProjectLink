Causes


In adults, lifestyle-related risk factors, such as smoking, being overweight, not getting enough exercise, eating an unhealthy diet etc. play a major role in many types of Cancer. But lifestyle factors usually take many years to cause cancer risk, and they are not known to play much role in childhood cancers.




  • A few environmental factors, such as the exposure of radiation, has been linked with some types of childhood cancers.
  • A research has also suggested that smoking tobacco might increase a child's risk of certain cancers, but more research is needed to explore these possible links.
  • Most cancers in children are caused when the alterations in genes is caused it leads to uncontrolled cell growth and eventually cancer.
  • Genetic changes that are passed from parents to their children—known as germline variants—can be linked with an increased risk of cancer.
  • Genetic changes that lead to the cancer can also occur spontaneously in the cells during development.








    • So far, most childhood cancers have not been shown to have the environmental causes but
    genetic changes remain a major cause to most common cancers like Leukemia and Brain tumors.



    Prevention


    Children's genes, the DNA that they inherit from their parents, influence their risk of cancer which is not something that can be changed. But lifestyle and environment at a smaller extent but still influence their risk, and there can be an 'Impact'.


  • Children should have a healthy diet with lots of fiber, fruits and vegetables. Processed meats and an overabundance of red meat and salt should be avoided.
  • Children should be encouraged to do. Exercise stabilizes levels of hormones like estrogen and insulin that have been linked to cancer.
  • Fatty tissues produce hormones that influence the way cells grow. Cell overgrowth is at the root of cancers. Hence children should be encouraged to have a healthy body weight.
  • Indoor pesticides have recently been found to increase children's risk of Leukemia by 47%.
  • Cancer causing chemicals such as arsenic, benzene, and asbestos are still used in textile industry, we should ban using those chemicals and come out with a replacement.
